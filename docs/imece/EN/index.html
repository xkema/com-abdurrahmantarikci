İMECE

İMECE is an ethno – jazz – fusion project of Abdurrahman Tarikci, based on Anatolian traditional music. This project can be defined as the search of the melodies of the mind and soul. This search sometimes could be limited with only Anatolian traditional music and sometimes could be combined with music of any part of the world.

ABDURRAHMAN TARİKCİ

Abdurrahman Tarikci was born in Yozgat in 1977. He graduated from Department of Physics Education, Middle East Technical University (METU) in 1988. After that, he got degree of M. of S. in 2002 and Ph. D. degree in 2010 at Physics Department at METU.

In addition to his academic works, he is bass player, singer, arranger and sound engineer. As a musician, he participated some international music festivals such as, "METU Jazz Days" (1996), "Akbank Jazz Festival" (1997,1998) Ankara Music Festival" (1998). Furthermore, he participated many TV programs as a member of orchestra by playing bas and bas bağlama. Some of these programs are "Türkü Türkü Türkiyem", "Bir Nefes Anadolu", "Üçü Bir Yerde", "Bergüzar", "Dem bu dem", "Tatlı Dile Güler Yüze", "Balkan Havası", "Türkü Söylemek Lazım", "Çalsın Davullar", and "Dem-i Devran". In addition, Tarikci was the music director of the many TV programs such as "Bir Hilal Uğruna Ya Rab-Çanakkale", "Düet Gecesi", "Türkülerle Yılbaşı", "Dem-i Devran", and İmece. Also, as a sound engineer he recorded, mixed and mastered lots of albums.

Now, Abdurrahman Tarikci is working at "Studyo TINI" as a sound engineer, producer and arranger. In addition, he is assistant professor at Department of Music of Çankırı Karatekin University, Fine Arts Faculty.
